Entering into grade 12, everyone I met had something to say. Whether it be encouraging me try hard and get good grades, or asking me about my future plans after highschool graduation, or congratulating me about getting this far at all (interesting one), I was struck by a particular piece of advice most people seemed to tell me...

>  *Enjoy your last year of high school!*

> *You'll cherish these memories, so don't forget to make some!*

>  *You're in grade 12? Great! Don't forget to relax and enjoy your last year before hell...*

It was as though I was approaching an invisible door (aka *graduation*) leading from the normal world of school to the unknown realms of nothingness. "Enjoy your time," people said. "It will be fun," people said. Well, that is yet to be determined.

## Outlook 

Contrary to the (presumable) norm, I always looked forward to school. Call it rosy retrospective, but I my early memories of school were generally tinted with an ever-present sense of excitement, enthusiasm, eagerness...

When people complained about school, showing up on Mondays with half-shut eyes, cursing the entire education system responsible for forcing them our of their beds, or drew out long cries of frustration the minute math or science was mentioned, or in general showed clear distaste at the idea of studying, I sort of... disagreed. Of course I did not make that apparent—God forbid I turn into one of those nerds who actually enjoy studying (what in the world?), but the fact of the matter remains: I enjoy learning. I enjoy school. I enjoy studying. And I believe there should be nothing wrong with that.

Likewise, I showed up to the first day of grade 12 excited, and—believe it or not—somewhat afraid that courses would not be starting on the first day. However, I was pleasantly surprised by the lesson on hydrocarbons in Chemistry class. It was a jolt, mind you, to suddenly start learning after spending a lazy summer out of the country, but a welcomed jolt nonetheless. A fascinating first lesson on organic compounds, alkanes, alkenes, alkynes, and the like. It was a preview of the challenging yet wonderful course that was SCH4U.

English class began the same as all years—with a lesson on topic, purpose, and audience. I practically have these things memorized. You know, this is one of those things I love about my English teacher. Being Old Reliable, his classes are always the same every year—no surprises. Wonderful.

Math class was a chaotic mess. Being in first period, we discovered that the chairs and desks for grade 12 were missing. "The new ones will come in October," they said. We ended up in the science lab for the first class of math... or you would think. Instead we went over the same rules and regulations that all teachers go over on the first day of school—rules that no one seemed to follow. In any case, we had Chemistry to look forward to.
